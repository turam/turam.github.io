Exploratory Research - Bicycle Commuting in Seattle

Project Overview

Team - Joanne Zih-Han Wang, Shreyas Gowda, Rahul Zende, Turam Purty
Our team was assigned the task of an exploratory study to identify and refine research problems that could help validate the various parameters on which Seattle has been consistently ranked as a top city for bicycling in USA, even though the bicycle adoption rate had been stagnant in the city for the last decade. Based on literature review, we formulated the problem statement of “What are the factors that inhibit bicycle commuting in Seattle?”. We conducted 9 interviews and solicited 42 responses from an online survey. Our primary analysis validated our initial assumptions and we recommended problem statements for further research in five key categories.

Impact Innovation Challenge 2019

Project Overview

Team - Ankit Rawat, Prasad Thakur, Divit Jawa, Noya Dolev, Turam Purty
Our team was given a design challenge to address the crisis of Homlessness in seattle during a 12-hour Impact Innovation Challenge at University of Washington. Through secondary research, we formulated the problem statement of “How can we provide immediate jobs and income opportunities to homeless people and give them stability?". We addressed this problem statement by designing a proof of concept platform, called JobSeekers, that could address three major causes of Homlessness in Seattle and help a significant homeless population in seattle. Our team was awarded 2nd place.
